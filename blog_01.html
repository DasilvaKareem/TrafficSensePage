Navigating Traffic Sense

Greetings, fellow innovators and enthusiasts! Today, I'm thrilled to share the remarkable journey of Traffic Sense, an IoT sensor set to revolutionize maritime safety at Stannard Rock. Join me as I recount our story, from its inception at Edesia.Catering's smart city hackathon to the upcoming Summer 2024 launch.

Inception: Project Sweet Spot

Originally named Project Sweet Spot, Traffic Sense emerged from a smart city hackathon in the heart of Memphis, Tennessee. Initially focused on aiding small businesses and retail owners in monitoring traffic flow, our vision evolved. The desire to empower owners in gauging foot traffic led to the birth of Traffic Sense—an IoT sensor designed for discreet activity monitoring without compromising user privacy.

The project faced a brief pause until our Chief Data Officer developed our first AI algorithm, predicting traffic flow. This breakthrough reignited the flames of innovation, propelling Traffic Sense forward.

Transformation: LaunchTN Innovation Week

Our journey took a transformative turn during LaunchTN Innovation Week in Nashville. This gathering of entrepreneurial minds aimed to empower Tennessee startups by fostering collaborations with esteemed government agencies like the NIH and NSF. It was during this event that we were introduced to the Michigan Clean Future Fund through the incredible Alison Beatty, marking the genesis of a partnership that would shape our project's trajectory.

Seafaring Innovation: Fresh Coast Maritime Challenge

A significant highlight in our saga was our participation in the Fresh Coast Maritime Challenge—an avant-garde program electrifying the waterways of Northwest Michigan. This initiative seamlessly aligned with Traffic Sense's commitment to innovation, positioning Michigan at the forefront of sustainable maritime transportation. During this exhilarating challenge, we had the privilege of meeting Travis White, a distinguished Research Engineer at Michigan Tech University's Great Lakes Research Center. Renowned for his work with autonomous marine vehicles, Travis shared in our excitement about Traffic Sense.

Business Incubation: Vanderbilt Ideator Program

Our journey continued with the Vanderbilt Ideator Program—a transformative business incubator leading to NSF I-Corps. This program not only facilitated our business development but also connected us with mentor companies like Darvis Ai. As finalists, we secured a grant from the Vanderbilt Ideator program, enabling us to complete the Traffic Sense prototype and initiate phase 1 testing in beautiful Michigan at Lake Superior.

Maritime Collaboration: Teaming Up with Travis White

Exciting news! We're teaming up with Travis White to deploy Traffic Sense for monitoring boat activity, enhancing maritime safety. Designed to withstand challenging weather conditions, our IoT sensor is set to revolutionize maritime operations.

As we gear up for the Summer 2024 launch, Stannard Rock takes center stage. Beyond its historical significance, the lighthouse becomes a beacon for climate research by universities, government agencies, and environmental groups.

Stay tuned for this innovative collaboration, making waves for safer and more sustainable maritime activities!
